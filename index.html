The Silent Patient

Alex Michaelides

Chapter 1

Alicia Berenson was thirty-three years old when she killed her husband. They had been married for seven years. They were both artists—Alicia was a painter, and Gabriel was a well-known fashion photographer. He had been forty years old at the time of his death.

I remember the day she was arrested. It was a Friday in August, just after lunch. I was in my office when I heard the news. I can't recall who told me—probably one of the nurses. I do remember standing up and walking over to the window, looking out at the garden below, trying to make sense of what I'd heard.

Alicia Berenson had shot her husband in the face five times. No one knew why. She hadn't spoken since that night. Not a single word.

That was six years ago.

Now, for the first time, Alicia Berenson is going to talk.

Chapter 2

I first heard about Alicia Berenson from a colleague at the Grove, a secure forensic unit in North London where I've worked for the past five years. I'm a forensic psychotherapist—I work with criminals who've been diagnosed with mental disorders. I assess them, treat them if possible, and then report to the courts on whether they're still a danger to society.

Most of my patients are violent offenders—murderers, rapists, arsonists. Some are psychopaths. Others are simply damaged individuals who've snapped under pressure. Alicia Berenson was different. She was a famous painter married to a famous photographer. They were the golden couple, beloved by the media. Until the night she shot him.
